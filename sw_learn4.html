The Baroque

Descartes

1596-1650

Rationalist and Dualist

(one who has an unshakable faith in human reason)

(one who believes in a two-fold reality)

Main Beliefs

Believed philosophy should go from simple to complex known as the "father of modern philosophy"

Believed in a mathematical approach to philosophy

Believed in a connection between thinking and being

Believed in two "substances"- thought and extension (mind and matter)

Philosophical Project

To achieve insight into the nature of man and the universe

To study the relationship between body and mind

Spinoza

1632-1677

Rationalist and Pantheist

(one who has an unshakable faith in human reason)

(one who believes that deity/God is present in all things)

Main Beliefs

Believed every individual is a piece of an enormous whole

Believed in a mathematical approach to philosophy

Questioned validity of certain religious texts

Believed thought and extension were one substance

Philosophical Project

To examine how religion fits into everything in life

Locke

1632-1704

Empiricist

(believes everything in the mind comes from the senses)

Main Beliefs

Believed we got our ideas from the senses

Believed we could only perceive "simple sensations"

Philosophical Project

To understand the human mind and its acquisition of knowledge

Hume

1711-1776

Empiricist

(believes everything in the mind comes from the senses)

Main Beliefs

Believed we had two types of perceptions: impressions and ideas

Opposed all thoughts that came from sense perceptions

Believed in law of causation

Philosophical Project

To prove the idea everything in the mind comes from the senses

Berkeley

1685-1753

Empiricist

(believes everything in the mind comes from the senses)

Main Beliefs

Believed current philosophers threatened the christian faith

Believed in "spirit" and "spiritual" ideas

Believed the only thing that exists are those we perceive

Believed we do not perceive matter or material

Philosophical Project

Attempted to answer the question: "who are we?"
