The Renaissance

Copernicus

1473-1543

Empiricist

(believes everything in the mind comes from the senses)

Main Beliefs

Believed in geocentric theory- the Earth revolved around the Sun

Believed the Sun was the center of the universe

Philosophical Project

To explain Earth’s relationship with the Sun and the universe

Kepler

1571-1630

Empiricist

(believes everything in the mind comes from the senses)

Main Beliefs

Studied and proved that planets have elliptical orbits

Believed the same laws applied to the rest of the universe

Thought there had to be some attraction between heavenly bodies

Philosophical Project

To explain the mysteries of space and the universe

Galileo Galilei

1564-1642

Empiricist

(believes everything in the mind comes from the senses)

Main Beliefs

Formulated the Law of Inertia

Claimed that the moon had mountains and valleys similar to those on earth

Discovered Jupiter’s moons

Philosophical Project

To study heavenly bodies and explain how they functioned

Newton

1473-1543

Empiricist

(believes everything in the mind comes from the senses)

Main Beliefs

Combined laws of gravity and inertia to explain the laws of planetary orbits

Philosophical Project

To explain why things fall back to earth and how planets stay in orbit in such a large universe
