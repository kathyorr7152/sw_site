Romanticism

Schelling

1775-1854

Idealist and Monist

(believes that reality is fundamentally constructed in the mind)

(one who reduces nature and the condition of all things into one single substance)

Main Beliefs

Wished to unite mind and matter

Believed nature is visible spirit and spirit is invisible nature

Philosophical Project

Attempting to explain that nature and reality belong as one in the mind

Schelling

1770-1831

Individualist

(one who believes that reality is different for each individual)

Main Beliefs

Believed all knowledge is human knowledge

Believed basis for human thought changes from generation to generation

Philosophical Project

Wanted to emphasize the importance of family, civil society, and state

Kierkegaard

1813-1855

Individualist

(one who believes that reality is different for each individual)

Main Beliefs

Believed the lives we are living today are devoid of commitment and passion

Believed Christianity was overwhelming and irrational

Believed we should not search for universal truths, but rather search for truths that pertain to an individual

Believed in the "3 stages of life": aesthetic stage (question if it’s fun), ethical stage (question if it’s right), and religious stage (choose faith)

Philosophical Project

To convince others that individualism is the correct view
